About

Engineering by training.
Increasingly focused on how energy systems behave.

I’m an Energy & Decarbonisation Engineer at Alpine Energy, where my work sits at the intersection of network behaviour, electrification, and distributed energy.

Through an EECA-funded programme, I work closely with large industrial energy users to understand how decarbonisation and electrification decisions translate into real network impacts — particularly at a feeder level. This involves assessing how load shifts, electrification pathways, and efficiency improvements interact with network constraints, while supporting organisations in shaping practical, end-to-end energy strategies.

Alongside this, I actively explore demand flexibility opportunities — identifying where load shaping, operational changes, or DER integration can create value for both the customer and the network, often as an alternative or complement to traditional infrastructure investment.

A key part of my work has evolved into building decision-support tools and analytical frameworks — including DER uptake forecasting models, solar ROI calculators, pricing comparison tools, and internal dashboards — all aimed at improving how decisions are made under uncertainty.

Before Alpine Energy, I held multiple roles at SolarZero, primarily as a Senior Solar Engineer, with secondments as Solar Engineering Manager and Energy Systems Engineer.

That experience grounded me in the delivery side of distributed energy — from designing high-volume residential solar systems and improving engineering processes, to diagnosing system performance issues through inverter and battery data, and working closely with operations, electricians, and customers to ensure successful installations.

It also gave me a practical understanding of how DER behaves outside models — how systems perform, where inefficiencies emerge, and how technical decisions translate into customer outcomes on scale.

Alongside my role, I run an independent consulting practice focused on DER strategy, electrification pathways, and energy decision-making — typically working at the boundary between technical feasibility and real-world implementation.

I’m also increasingly interested in how AI and software can improve the way the energy industry operates — from automating analysis to enabling better, faster, and more consistent decision-making.

Values & How I Think

A foundation for decisions
in complex systems.

I don’t see energy problems as purely technical. Most of the challenges I’ve worked on sit at the intersection of network constraints, customer behaviour, and evolving system dynamics. The technical answer is rarely the full answer — it has to work in context.

I tend to focus on understanding how things behave in reality before trying to optimise them — especially when it comes to distributed energy, electrification, and demand.

Practical Over Perfect
I prioritise solutions that can actually be implemented within real constraints — whether that’s customer behaviour, network limitations, or operational realities. A good solution that works is more valuable than a perfect one that doesn’t.
Understanding the System Before Optimising It
I try to understand how things behave in reality before attempting to improve them — especially in areas like distributed energy, electrification, and demand flexibility where interactions matter more than isolated components.
Lateral Thinking Where It Matters
I don’t assume the best solutions always come from established approaches. As the system evolves, even small shifts in thinking can unlock meaningful improvements — particularly in how we use DER, flexibility, and data.
Using Data to Improve Decisions, Not Just Explain Them
I’m interested in building tools and analysis that help people make better decisions — not just dashboards that describe what has already happened.
AI as a Practical Tool, Not a Concept
I use AI where it improves speed, clarity, or consistency in analysis — especially in automating workflows and reducing friction in repetitive tasks.
01
Systems Over Isolated Optimisation
Energy systems don’t operate in silos. What looks optimal in one part of the system can create problems elsewhere. I try to understand the interactions before suggesting solutions.
02
What Works in Practice Matters More Than What Works in Theory
Good ideas don’t always survive real-world constraints. I’m more interested in solutions that are slightly imperfect but implementable than ones that look ideal on paper.
03
Clarity Improves Decisions
A lot of complexity in energy comes from lack of visibility. I like building tools and analysis that help people see what’s actually happening — and make better decisions because of it.
04
The System Is Changing Faster Than the Models
With DER, electrification, and flexibility, the system is evolving quickly. I’m interested in staying close to that change — and understanding how behaviour shifts before it becomes obvious.
05
There’s Value in Being Able to Translate
Some of the most useful work happens between domains — technical, commercial, operational. I try to operate in that space, where different perspectives need to come together.
